Not all texts are created equal.

If you’ve ever read a poem, play, novel, or some other “literary” work, you probably read only one version of the text. But most of the time, there are many versions of the same work, with slight differences in word choice, sentence structure, and spelling.

Often the text you see in a nice, published book is actually an amalgamation of multiple original texts, with the choices often being made by academics known as textual critics.

Why would there be different versions of a work? Sometimes, an author might choose to revise a work multiple times as his or her artistic sensibilities shift. Editors might also modify a text, removing perceived obscenity or even creatively inserting their own material. And since most of these texts were written before computers, or even the printing press, errors could be introduced when people copied the work for themselves.

Why does this matter? Because words matter, and sometimes a single word variant can change an entire interpretation of a text.


Read a text along with all its variants.

Variant lets you read a text just like you would anywhere else, but it also makes it easy for you to see a text’s variants, as well as words that are particularly variable.

When you view a text, Variant highlights words that have many variants. You can also click on a word, and Variant will give you all of the words in the variant texts that it thinks the word corresponds to. If you uploaded that text, you can choose which version of the word you want reflected in the base text.

Variant makes collation easier.

Variant was designed to help make the collation process easier for textual critics. When you upload a base text, Variant will also let you upload variant texts. Variant will automatically take care of the normally tedious process of comparing each word of the variant text to each word of the base text.

If you don’t like a match that Variant made, simply view the variant text side-by-side with the base text, and drag a word from the variant text over a word from the base text to make a match. Variant will also highlight matches between very different words, so you know where to look when collating.
