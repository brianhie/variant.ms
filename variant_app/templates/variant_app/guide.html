Textual collation in four easy steps.

Step 1: Upload a base text.

  1. First, login to Variant. If you don’t have an account, you can register here.
  2. Next, click on the My Home tab on the top left of the screen.
  3. Under My Texts, click on the + Add a new text link.
  4. You should see a form that looks like this:



    The text content you enter here will be the version of the text that gets compared with each variant text you add in the future, which is why we refer to it as the base text.
  5. Click on the Submit button to add the base text!

Step 2: Upload variant texts.

  1. Once you’ve added a base text, you should see an icon with the text’s name show up under My Texts:



    Click on that icon to go to that text’s home page.
  2. To add a variant, click on the + Add a variant link.
  3. You should see a form similar to the one you used to upload a base text. Add the variant text’s content here:



  4. Click on the Collate button to collate the variant text with the base text. Depending on the size of your text, this could take up to a couple minutes, during which you should not refresh or close the browser window.

Step 3: Make manual collation changes (if needed).

  1. In the home page for you text, click on the light blue icon with the name you provided for your variant.
  2. Select the Compare with base text checkbox (if it is not already checked by default) to view the variant text and the base text side-by-side.
  3. Click the Highlight variable words checkbox to highlight places where Variant matched two very different words.
  4. Sometimes, Variant’s matches do not correspond to actual manuscript variants, but are due to quirks in our matching procedure. To make a manual match, just click on a word in the variant text, then click on the corresponding word in the base text. That’s it!



  5. If you want to match a sequence of words from the variant text to a single word in the base text, just click on the first and last words in the sequence and then click on the corresponding word in the base text.



  6. You can also make a match between words by dragging the variant word over the base text word.



Step 4: Choose which variant to use in the collated text.

  1. Back on the home page for your text, click the dark blue View collated text icon.
  2. Click on a word and a list of variants should show up on the right side of the page:



  3. If you want to use another variant in the collated text, just click on that variant and the changes should be saved automatically.

That’s it!

You can repeat the above steps to collate additional texts and variants, and Variant provides and easy and beautiful way to share and visualize your hard collation work. Happy collating!
